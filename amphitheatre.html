AMPHITHEATRE

Historic Roman amphitheatres are ancient structures built in the shape of an oval or circle, with seating arranged in tiers around a central arena. The amphitheatre is a symbol of Roman engineering and architecture, and many were used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and other events. The most famous example is the Colosseum in Rome.
